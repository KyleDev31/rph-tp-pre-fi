The 1899 Malolos Constitution

The 1899 Malolos Constitution is the first republic of the Philippines. Emilio Aguinaldo as a member of the Katipunan fully ratified the declaration against the Spanish ruling to gain independence of the Filipinos from the abuse of Spaniards. The 1899 Constitution has its preamble which is "We, The representatives of the Filipino people, lawfully convened, in order to establish justice, provide for common defense, promote the general welfare, and insure the benefits of liberty, imploring the aid of the Sovereign Legislator of the Universe for the attainment of these ends, have voted, decreed, and sanctioned the following:".

The Constitution of 1899 has 14 articles within it, which are The Republic, The Government, Religion, The Filipinos and their National Individual Rights, The Legislative Power, The Permanent Commission, The Executive Power, The President of the Republic, The Secretaries of the Government, The Judicial Power, Provincial and Popular Assemblies, Administration of the State, Amendment of the Constitution, and Constitutional Observance, Oath, and Language. This constitutional articles solidified the 1899 constitution which gave rights and freedom to the people of the republic of the Philippines.

Before the promulgation of the constitution on January 22, 1899, it is important to note that the First Philippine Republic faced challenges and conflicts, including the Philippine-American War, which ultimately led to the annexation of the Philippines by the United States in 1901. As a result, the Malolos Constitution had a relatively short-lived impact, but it remains an important historical document in the Philippines. Because it became a constitution to make the Filipino people in-charge of its own country and not colonizers despite the short period of time before the Americans and Japanese began to colonize the Philippines.
